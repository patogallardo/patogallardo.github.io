Who am I

I am a physicist, I am a fellow at the Kavli Institute for Cosmological Physics (KICP) at the University of Chicago. I design, analyze and build instruments that observe the cosmic microwave background, the oldest light in the universe. I also work measuring the movements of the largest gravitationally bound structures in the unvierse using the kinematic Sunyaev-Zeldovich effect.

Work

My work focuses on planning, designing and evaluating next-generation cosmic microwave background experiments like CMB-S4 and the Simons Observatory.

I currently work on the optical design of the large-aperture telescopes and cameras for CMB-S4, a proposed large-scale CMB observatory. I have contributed to the design of the South Pole large-aperture telescope for CMB-S4 and its array of optics tubes and I am working on the optical design of the Chile large aperture telescopes optics.

In addition to my contribution to CMB-S4, I study the movements of galaxies in very large distances. I use these observables to test models of gravity to answer fundamental questions about the kinematics of our universe.
